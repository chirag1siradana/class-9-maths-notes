# Class 9 Maths – Key Concepts Overview

This file provides a bird's-eye view of the core mathematical concepts covered across all 15 chapters of Class 9 Mathematics.

---

## Number Systems
- **Real numbers** = Rational ∪ Irrational
- Rational: p/q form | Irrational: non-terminating, non-repeating
- Key identities for surds and rationalisation
- Laws of exponents: aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ, (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ, a⁰ = 1

---

## Polynomials
- Degree determines type (linear, quadratic, cubic, …)
- **Remainder Theorem**: remainder of p(x) ÷ (x−a) is p(a)
- **Factor Theorem**: (x−a) is a factor ⟺ p(a) = 0
- Key identities: (a±b)², (a±b)³, a²−b², a³±b³, (a+b+c)²

---

## Coordinate Geometry
- Cartesian plane: x-axis (horizontal) + y-axis (vertical)
- Point = ordered pair (x, y); four quadrants by sign of coordinates
- Axes: x-axis → (a, 0); y-axis → (0, b)

---

## Linear Equations in Two Variables
- Standard form: ax + by + c = 0
- Infinitely many solutions; each solution is a point on a straight line
- Special cases: x = a (vertical line), y = b (horizontal line)

---

## Euclid's Geometry
- Built on undefined terms, definitions, axioms (general truths), and postulates (geometric rules)
- 5 Postulates; Postulate 5 is the Parallel Postulate
- Theorems derived by logical proof

---

## Lines and Angles
- Complementary (90°), Supplementary (180°), Vertically opposite (equal)
- Parallel lines + transversal: corresponding / alternate angles equal; co-interior supplementary
- Angle sum of triangle = 180°

---

## Triangles
- Congruence criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, RHS
- Isosceles triangle: equal sides ↔ equal opposite angles
- Triangle inequality: a + b > c for all side combinations

---

## Quadrilaterals
- Angle sum = 360°
- Parallelogram: opposite sides/angles equal; diagonals bisect each other
- Special parallelograms: Rectangle (equal diagonals), Rhombus (⊥ diagonals), Square (both)
- Mid-Point Theorem: midpoint segment ∥ third side and = ½ of it

---

## Areas of Parallelograms and Triangles
- Same base + same parallels → equal area
- Area(triangle) = ½ × Area(parallelogram) with same base and parallels
- Median divides triangle into two equal-area parts

---

## Circles
- Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle (same arc)
- Angles in same segment are equal
- Perpendicular from centre bisects chord (and converse)
- Cyclic quadrilateral: opposite angles sum to 180°

---

## Constructions
- Tools: compass + straightedge only
- Construct perpendicular bisector, angle bisector
- Construct angles: 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, 120°
- Construct triangles given SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS conditions

---

## Heron's Formula
- s = (a + b + c) / 2
- Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]
- Equilateral triangle: Area = (√3/4)a²
- Apply to quadrilaterals by splitting into two triangles

---

## Surface Areas and Volumes

| Solid | TSA | Volume |
|-------|-----|--------|
| Cuboid | 2(lb+bh+hl) | lbh |
| Cube | 6a² | a³ |
| Cylinder | 2πr(r+h) | πr²h |
| Cone | πr(r+l) | ⅓πr²h |
| Sphere | 4πr² | (4/3)πr³ |
| Hemisphere | 3πr² | (2/3)πr³ |

---

## Statistics
- Mean = Σx/n (ungrouped)
- Median: middle value after arranging in order
- Mode: most frequently occurring value
- Representations: bar graph, histogram, frequency polygon

---

## Probability
- P(E) = (favourable trials) / (total trials)
- 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1; P(E) + P(not E) = 1
- P = 0 → impossible event; P = 1 → certain event
