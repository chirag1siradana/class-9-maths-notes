# Chapter 1: Number Systems

## Overview

This chapter introduces the real number system, including rational and irrational numbers, and their properties.

---

## Key Concepts

### 1.1 Rational Numbers
- A number that can be expressed in the form **p/q**, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.
- Examples: 1/2, −3/4, 7, 0.333...

### 1.2 Irrational Numbers
- A number that **cannot** be expressed as p/q (non-terminating, non-repeating decimals).
- Examples: √2, √3, π, e

### 1.3 Real Numbers
- The collection of all rational and irrational numbers.
- Represented on the **number line**.

### 1.4 Representing Real Numbers on the Number Line
- Every real number has a unique point on the number line.
- Irrational numbers such as √2 can be located using geometric construction.

### 1.5 Operations on Real Numbers
- Real numbers are closed under addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division (except by zero).
- **Rationalising the denominator**: multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate to remove irrational numbers from the denominator.

### 1.6 Laws of Exponents for Real Numbers

| Law | Expression |
|-----|------------|
| Product Rule | aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ |
| Quotient Rule | aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ |
| Power Rule | (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ |
| Zero Exponent | a⁰ = 1 (a ≠ 0) |
| Negative Exponent | a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ |

---

## Important Formulas

- √(a × b) = √a × √b
- √(a/b) = √a / √b
- (√a + √b)(√a − √b) = a − b
- (a + √b)(a − √b) = a² − b

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** Simplify (2 + √3)(2 − √3)
> = 4 − 3 = **1**

**Example 2:** Rationalise 1/(√2 + 1)
> Multiply by (√2 − 1)/(√2 − 1) = (√2 − 1)/(2 − 1) = **√2 − 1**

---

## Summary

- Rational numbers: p/q form; Irrational: non-terminating, non-repeating
- Together they form the set of **Real Numbers (ℝ)**
- Key identity: (√a + √b)(√a − √b) = a − b
- Laws of exponents apply to all real-number bases
