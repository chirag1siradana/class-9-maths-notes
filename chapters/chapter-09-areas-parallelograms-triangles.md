# Chapter 9: Areas of Parallelograms and Triangles

## Overview

This chapter examines the relationship between areas of parallelograms and triangles that share the same base and lie between the same parallel lines.

---

## Key Concepts

### 9.1 Figures on the Same Base and Between the Same Parallels
- Two figures are said to be on the **same base** if they share a common side.
- They are between the **same parallels** if the base lies on one line and the opposite vertices/side lies on a parallel line.

### 9.2 Parallelograms on the Same Base
- **Theorem:** Parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallels are **equal in area**.
- Area of parallelogram = **Base × Height**

### 9.3 Triangles on the Same Base
- **Theorem:** Triangles on the same base and between the same parallels are **equal in area**.
- Area of triangle = **½ × Base × Height**

### 9.4 Relationship Between Parallelogram and Triangle
- **Theorem:** If a triangle and a parallelogram are on the same base and between the same parallels, then area of triangle = **½ × area of parallelogram**.

### 9.5 Median of a Triangle
- A median divides a triangle into **two triangles of equal area**.

---

## Important Formulas

| Figure | Area Formula |
|--------|-------------|
| Parallelogram | Base × Height |
| Triangle | ½ × Base × Height |
| Rectangle | Length × Breadth |
| Square | Side² |
| Rhombus | ½ × d₁ × d₂ |
| Trapezium | ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × Height |

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** A parallelogram has a base of 10 cm and height 6 cm. Find its area.
> Area = 10 × 6 = **60 cm²**

**Example 2:** A triangle has the same base and height as the parallelogram above. Find its area.
> Area = ½ × 10 × 6 = **30 cm²** (= ½ × area of parallelogram)

**Example 3:** In △ABC, D is the midpoint of BC. Show that ar(ABD) = ar(ACD).
> AD is the median → it divides △ABC into two equal areas.

---

## Practice Problems

1. Two parallelograms ABCD and EFCD share base CD and lie between the same parallels CD ∥ AE. Prove that ar(ABCD) = ar(EFCD).
2. In △PQR, S is the midpoint of QR. Prove that ar(△PQS) = ar(△PSR).
3. A parallelogram has area 48 cm². A triangle is on the same base and between the same parallels. Find the area of the triangle.
4. Calculate the area of a trapezium with parallel sides 8 cm and 12 cm, and height 5 cm.
5. Prove that a diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two triangles of equal area.

---

## Summary

- Equal areas: parallelograms (or triangles) on the same base between the same parallels
- Area of triangle = ½ × area of parallelogram (same base and parallels)
- Median divides a triangle into two equal-area triangles
