# Chapter 11: Constructions

## Overview

This chapter covers geometric constructions using a compass and straightedge, including bisectors and specific angles.

---

## Key Concepts

### 11.1 Basic Constructions

#### Construction 1: Bisect a Given Angle
**Steps:**
1. Draw angle ∠ABC.
2. With B as centre, draw an arc cutting BA at P and BC at Q.
3. With P and Q as centres, draw equal arcs intersecting at R.
4. Join BR. BR is the angle bisector.

#### Construction 2: Perpendicular Bisector of a Line Segment
**Steps:**
1. Draw line segment AB.
2. With A as centre and radius > ½AB, draw arcs on both sides.
3. Repeat with B as centre (same radius).
4. Join the two intersection points. This is the perpendicular bisector.

### 11.2 Constructing Special Angles

| Angle | Method |
|-------|--------|
| 60° | Equilateral triangle construction |
| 90° | Perpendicular bisector |
| 45° | Bisect 90° |
| 30° | Bisect 60° |
| 120° | Extend one side of 60° triangle |

#### Construction of 60°:
1. Draw ray AB.
2. With A as centre, draw an arc cutting AB at P.
3. With P as centre and same radius, mark Q on the arc.
4. Join AQ. ∠QAB = 60°.

### 11.3 Constructing a Triangle
Given conditions for a unique triangle:
- **SSS**: Three sides
- **SAS**: Two sides and included angle
- **ASA**: Two angles and included side
- **RHS**: Hypotenuse and one side (right triangle)

#### Construction (SSS Example – sides 4 cm, 5 cm, 6 cm):
1. Draw base BC = 5 cm.
2. With B as centre and radius 4 cm, draw an arc.
3. With C as centre and radius 6 cm, draw another arc.
4. Mark their intersection as A. Join AB and AC.

## Examples

**Example 1:** Construct ∠45°.
> Construct 90° first (perpendicular bisector), then bisect it to get 45°.

**Example 2:** Construct △PQR where PQ = 6 cm, QR = 5 cm, RP = 4 cm.
> Use SSS construction method.

## Practice Problems

1. Construct a perpendicular bisector of a line segment of length 7 cm.
2. Construct ∠60° and verify by measuring.
3. Construct △ABC with AB = 5 cm, ∠A = 60°, AC = 4 cm.
4. Construct an isosceles triangle with base 6 cm and equal sides of 5 cm each.
5. Bisect an angle of 120° to get 60°.

---

## Summary

- Constructions use only compass and straightedge (ruler without markings for measurement)
- Perpendicular bisector and angle bisector are foundational constructions
- Special angles (30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, 120°) can all be constructed geometrically
- Triangle construction depends on given information (SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS)
