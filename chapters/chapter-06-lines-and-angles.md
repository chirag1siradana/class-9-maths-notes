# Chapter 6: Lines and Angles

## Overview

This chapter studies properties of lines, angles formed when lines intersect or are cut by a transversal, and related theorems.

---

## Key Concepts

### 6.1 Basic Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Line segment | Part of a line with two endpoints |
| Ray | Part of a line with one endpoint, extending infinitely in one direction |
| Collinear points | Points lying on the same line |
| Angle | Formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint (vertex) |

### 6.2 Types of Angles

| Type | Measure |
|------|---------|
| Acute | 0° < θ < 90° |
| Right | θ = 90° |
| Obtuse | 90° < θ < 180° |
| Straight | θ = 180° |
| Reflex | 180° < θ < 360° |

### 6.3 Pairs of Angles
- **Complementary**: sum = 90°
- **Supplementary**: sum = 180°
- **Adjacent**: share a common vertex and arm
- **Vertically opposite**: formed by two intersecting lines; always equal

### 6.4 Parallel Lines and a Transversal
When a transversal crosses two parallel lines:

| Angle Pair | Relationship |
|------------|-------------|
| Corresponding angles | Equal |
| Alternate interior angles | Equal |
| Alternate exterior angles | Equal |
| Co-interior (same-side interior) angles | Supplementary (sum = 180°) |

### 6.5 Lines Parallel to the Same Line
- If two lines are each parallel to a third line, they are parallel to each other.

### 6.6 Angle Sum Property of a Triangle
- The sum of angles in a triangle = **180°**.

---

## Important Theorems

1. If two lines intersect, vertically opposite angles are equal.
2. If a transversal intersects two parallel lines, corresponding angles are equal (and converse).
3. Lines parallel to the same line are parallel to each other.
4. The sum of angles of a triangle is 180°.

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** Two angles are supplementary. One angle is 70°. Find the other.
> 180° − 70° = **110°**

**Example 2:** A transversal cuts two parallel lines. If one alternate interior angle is 55°, find the other.
> **55°** (alternate interior angles are equal)

---

## Summary

- Complementary: 90°; Supplementary: 180°; Vertically opposite: equal
- Parallel lines cut by transversal → corresponding, alternate angles equal; co-interior supplementary
- Angle sum of triangle = 180°
