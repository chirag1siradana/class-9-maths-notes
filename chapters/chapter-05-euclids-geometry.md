# Chapter 5: Introduction to Euclid's Geometry

## Overview

This chapter introduces the foundations of geometry through Euclid's axioms, postulates, and theorems.

---

## Key Concepts

### 5.1 Euclid's Definitions (Selected)
- A **point** has no part.
- A **line** is breadthless length.
- The ends of a line are **points**.
- A **straight line** lies evenly with the points on itself.
- A **surface** has length and breadth only.

### 5.2 Euclid's Axioms
1. Things which are equal to the same thing are equal to each other.
2. If equals are added to equals, the wholes are equal.
3. If equals are subtracted from equals, the remainders are equal.
4. Things which coincide with one another are equal to one another.
5. The whole is greater than the part.

### 5.3 Euclid's Postulates
1. A straight line may be drawn from any one point to any other point.
2. A terminated line can be produced indefinitely.
3. A circle can be drawn with any centre and any radius.
4. All right angles are equal to one another.
5. If a straight line falling on two straight lines makes the interior angles on the same side less than two right angles, the two straight lines, if produced indefinitely, meet on that side.

### 5.4 Theorems
- Statements that are proved using definitions, axioms, postulates, and previously proved statements.

### 5.5 Consistent and Inconsistent Systems
- **Consistent**: a system with at least one solution.
- **Inconsistent**: a system with no solution (contradictory axioms).

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** If AB = PQ and PQ = XY, what can you conclude?
> By Euclid's first axiom: **AB = XY**

**Example 2:** If AC = BD and AC = 10 cm, what is BD?
> **BD = 10 cm** (things equal to the same thing are equal to each other)

## Summary

- Euclid organised geometry using undefined terms, definitions, axioms, and postulates.
- Axioms: general truths; Postulates: geometric-specific assumptions.
- Postulate 5 (Parallel Postulate) is the basis for Euclidean geometry.
- Theorems are derived by logical reasoning from axioms and postulates.
