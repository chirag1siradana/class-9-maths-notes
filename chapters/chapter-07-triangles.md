# Chapter 7: Triangles

## Overview

This chapter covers congruence of triangles, criteria for congruence, and properties of triangles.

---

## Key Concepts

### 7.1 Congruent Figures
- Two figures are **congruent** if they have the same shape and size.
- For congruent triangles: corresponding sides and angles are equal.
- Notation: △ABC ≅ △DEF

### 7.2 Criteria for Congruence of Triangles

| Criterion | Condition |
|-----------|-----------|
| SSS | All three sides are equal |
| SAS | Two sides and the included angle are equal |
| ASA | Two angles and the included side are equal |
| AAS | Two angles and a non-included side are equal |
| RHS | Right angle, hypotenuse, and one side are equal (right triangles only) |

### 7.3 Properties of Isosceles Triangles
- If two sides of a triangle are equal, the angles opposite them are equal. (**Isosceles Triangle Theorem**)
- Converse: If two angles of a triangle are equal, the sides opposite them are equal.

### 7.4 Inequalities in a Triangle
- In a triangle, the side opposite the larger angle is longer.
- The sum of any two sides is greater than the third side (**Triangle Inequality**).

---

## Important Theorems

1. **Theorem:** Angles opposite to equal sides of an isosceles triangle are equal.
2. **Theorem:** The sides opposite to equal angles of a triangle are equal.
3. **Theorem:** If two sides of a triangle are unequal, the angle opposite the longer side is larger.
4. **Theorem:** The sum of any two sides of a triangle is greater than the third side.

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** In △ABC and △DEF, AB = DE, BC = EF, ∠B = ∠E. Are the triangles congruent?
> Yes, by **SAS criterion**: △ABC ≅ △DEF

**Example 2:** In isosceles △PQR where PQ = PR, if ∠Q = 65°, find ∠R.
> Since PQ = PR, ∠Q = ∠R = **65°**

## Practice Problems

1. In △ABC, AB = AC and ∠A = 50°. Find ∠B and ∠C.
2. Prove that the medians of an equilateral triangle are equal.
3. In △PQR and △XYZ: PQ = XY, QR = YZ, ∠Q = ∠Y. Name the congruence criterion.
4. Can a triangle have sides of lengths 3, 4, and 8? Give reason.
5. In △ABC, ∠B = 40° and ∠C = 70°. Which side is the longest?

---

## Summary

- Congruence: same shape and size; notation △ABC ≅ △DEF
- Criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, RHS
- Isosceles triangle: equal sides ↔ equal opposite angles
- Triangle inequality: sum of two sides > third side
