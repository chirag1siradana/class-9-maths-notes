# Chapter 13: Surface Areas and Volumes

## Overview

This chapter covers the surface area and volume of three-dimensional solids including cuboids, cubes, cylinders, cones, spheres, and hemispheres.

---

## Key Concepts

### 13.1 Cuboid
- **Lateral (curved) surface area** = 2h(l + b)
- **Total surface area** = 2(lb + bh + hl)
- **Volume** = l × b × h

### 13.2 Cube (side = a)
- **Lateral surface area** = 4a²
- **Total surface area** = 6a²
- **Volume** = a³

### 13.3 Right Circular Cylinder (radius r, height h)
- **Curved surface area (CSA)** = 2πrh
- **Total surface area (TSA)** = 2πr(r + h)
- **Volume** = πr²h

### 13.4 Right Circular Cone (radius r, height h, slant height l)
- Slant height: l = √(r² + h²)
- **Curved surface area** = πrl
- **Total surface area** = πr(r + l)
- **Volume** = ⅓πr²h

### 13.5 Sphere (radius r)
- **Surface area** = 4πr²
- **Volume** = (4/3)πr³

### 13.6 Hemisphere (radius r)
- **Curved surface area** = 2πr²
- **Total surface area** = 3πr²
- **Volume** = (2/3)πr³

## Examples

**Example 1:** Find the volume of a cylinder with r = 7 cm, h = 10 cm.
> V = π × 7² × 10 = 490π ≈ **1539.4 cm³**

**Example 2:** Find the total surface area of a cone with r = 3 cm, h = 4 cm.
> l = √(9 + 16) = 5 cm
> TSA = π × 3 × (3 + 5) = 24π ≈ **75.4 cm²**

**Example 3:** A sphere has radius 6 cm. Find its volume.
> V = (4/3)π(6)³ = (4/3)π × 216 = 288π ≈ **904.8 cm³**

## Practice Problems

1. Find the lateral and total surface area of a cuboid: l = 10 cm, b = 8 cm, h = 5 cm.
2. A cylindrical tank has r = 3.5 m and h = 10 m. Find its capacity in litres.
3. A cone has base radius 5 cm and slant height 13 cm. Find its curved surface area.
4. Find the surface area and volume of a sphere with diameter 14 cm.
5. A hemispherical bowl has radius 10 cm. Find its curved surface area.

---

## Summary

- Use π ≈ 22/7 or 3.14 as appropriate.
- Slant height of cone: l = √(r² + h²)
- For combined solids, add/subtract relevant surface areas and volumes.
- 1 m³ = 1000 litres
