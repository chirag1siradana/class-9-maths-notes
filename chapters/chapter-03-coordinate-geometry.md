# Chapter 3: Coordinate Geometry

## Overview

This chapter introduces the Cartesian coordinate system and how to locate points in a plane using ordered pairs.

---

## Key Concepts

### 3.1 The Cartesian Plane
- Consists of two perpendicular number lines: the **x-axis** (horizontal) and **y-axis** (vertical).
- Their intersection is called the **origin O = (0, 0)**.

### 3.2 Quadrants

| Quadrant | x-sign | y-sign | Example |
|----------|--------|--------|---------|
| I | + | + | (3, 4) |
| II | − | + | (−2, 5) |
| III | − | − | (−1, −3) |
| IV | + | − | (4, −2) |

### 3.3 Coordinates of a Point
- An ordered pair **(x, y)** uniquely identifies a point in the plane.
- **x-coordinate (abscissa)**: horizontal distance from the y-axis.
- **y-coordinate (ordinate)**: vertical distance from the x-axis.

### 3.4 Points on the Axes
- Points on the x-axis have y = 0 → form (a, 0).
- Points on the y-axis have x = 0 → form (0, b).

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** Plot the point (−3, 2).
> Move 3 units left from origin along x-axis, then 2 units up → **Quadrant II**

**Example 2:** Identify the quadrant for (−4, −7).
> Both coordinates are negative → **Quadrant III**

**Example 3:** What are the coordinates of a point on the x-axis that is 5 units to the right of the origin?
> **(5, 0)**

---

## Practice Problems

1. Plot the following points on a coordinate plane: A(2, 3), B(−1, 4), C(−3, −2), D(4, −1).
2. In which quadrant or on which axis do these points lie: (0, −5), (−3, 0), (2, 7), (−1, −8)?
3. The point (a, b) lies in the third quadrant. What are the signs of a and b?
4. Find the mirror image of (3, −4) in the x-axis.
5. A point is equidistant from the x-axis and y-axis. If it lies in the second quadrant, write its coordinates in terms of a.

---

## Summary

- Cartesian plane: x-axis (horizontal), y-axis (vertical), origin (0, 0)
- A point is denoted by ordered pair (x, y)
- Four quadrants determined by signs of coordinates
- Points on axes: x-axis → (a, 0); y-axis → (0, b)
