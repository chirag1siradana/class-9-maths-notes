# Chapter 15: Probability

## Overview

This chapter introduces the concept of probability based on observations and experiments (empirical probability).

---

## Key Concepts

### 15.1 Experimental (Empirical) Probability
- Based on actual experiments and observations.
- **Formula:**

> P(event E) = (Number of trials in which event E occurred) / (Total number of trials)

- Probability always lies between **0 and 1** (inclusive):
  > 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1

### 15.2 Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Experiment | An action with an observable outcome |
| Trial | Each performance of an experiment |
| Event | A specific outcome or set of outcomes |
| Favourable outcome | Outcome(s) that satisfy the event |

### 15.3 Complementary Events
- If P(E) is the probability of event E happening:
  > P(not E) = 1 − P(E)
- P(E) + P(not E) = 1

### 15.4 Impossible and Certain Events
- **Impossible event**: P = 0 (e.g., rolling a 7 on a standard die)
- **Certain event**: P = 1 (e.g., getting a number ≤ 6 on a standard die)

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** A coin is tossed 100 times; heads appear 58 times. Find P(heads).
> P(heads) = 58/100 = **0.58**

**Example 2:** A die is rolled 300 times; 4 appears 52 times. Find P(not 4).
> P(4) = 52/300
> P(not 4) = 1 − 52/300 = 248/300 ≈ **0.827**

**Example 3:** From 50 trials, event A occurred 20 times. Find P(A) and P(not A).
> P(A) = 20/50 = 0.4
> P(not A) = 1 − 0.4 = **0.6**

## Practice Problems

1. A bag contains 5 red and 3 blue balls. If one ball is picked randomly in 80 trials and red appears 45 times, find P(red) experimentally.
2. A die is thrown 200 times with the following results:

| Outcome | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|---------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 38 | 32 | 29 | 40 | 36 | 25 |

Find the probability of getting: (a) a 3, (b) an odd number.

3. Two coins are tossed 500 times. Two heads appear 125 times, one head appears 275 times, and no head appears 100 times. Find the probability of each event.
4. Can the probability of an event be 1.2? Give reason.
5. If P(E) = 0.35, what is P(not E)?

---

## Summary

- Empirical probability: P(E) = (favourable trials) / (total trials)
- 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1 always
- P(E) + P(not E) = 1
- Impossible event: P = 0; Certain event: P = 1
- Based on observation/experiment, not theoretical assumption
