# Chapter 8: Quadrilaterals

## Overview

This chapter studies properties of quadrilaterals, including the angle sum property and special quadrilaterals.

---

## Key Concepts

### 8.1 Quadrilateral
- A polygon with **four sides**, four angles, and four vertices.
- Sum of interior angles of a quadrilateral = **360°**.

### 8.2 Types of Quadrilaterals

| Type | Properties |
|------|-----------|
| Parallelogram | Opposite sides parallel and equal; opposite angles equal |
| Rectangle | Parallelogram with all right angles; diagonals equal |
| Rhombus | Parallelogram with all sides equal; diagonals bisect at 90° |
| Square | All sides equal and all angles 90°; diagonals equal and bisect at 90° |
| Trapezium | Exactly one pair of opposite sides parallel |
| Kite | Two pairs of adjacent sides equal |

### 8.3 Properties of a Parallelogram
- Opposite sides are equal: AB = CD, BC = AD
- Opposite angles are equal: ∠A = ∠C, ∠B = ∠D
- Consecutive angles are supplementary: ∠A + ∠B = 180°
- Diagonals bisect each other.

### 8.4 Conditions for a Parallelogram
A quadrilateral is a parallelogram if:
- Both pairs of opposite sides are equal, OR
- Both pairs of opposite angles are equal, OR
- Diagonals bisect each other, OR
- One pair of opposite sides is both parallel and equal.

### 8.5 Mid-Point Theorem
- The line segment joining the midpoints of two sides of a triangle is **parallel** to the third side and **half** its length.

---

## Important Theorems

1. A diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two congruent triangles.
2. Opposite sides and angles of a parallelogram are equal.
3. Diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other.
4. Diagonals of a rectangle are equal.
5. Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles.

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = 75°. Find all other angles.
> ∠C = 75° (opposite); ∠B = ∠D = 105° (supplementary)

**Example 2:** In △PQR, M and N are midpoints of PQ and PR. If QR = 14 cm, find MN.
> By Mid-Point Theorem: MN = QR/2 = **7 cm**

---

## Practice Problems

1. In quadrilateral ABCD, ∠A = 80°, ∠B = 100°, ∠C = 70°. Find ∠D.
2. Prove that the diagonals of a rectangle are equal.
3. ABCD is a rhombus with diagonal AC = 8 cm and BD = 6 cm. Find the side of the rhombus.
4. In △ABC, D and E are midpoints of AB and AC. If DE = 5 cm, find BC.
5. Show that in a square, the diagonals are equal and bisect each other at right angles.

---

## Summary

- Angle sum of quadrilateral = 360°
- Parallelogram: opposite sides/angles equal; diagonals bisect each other
- Rectangle/Rhombus/Square are special parallelograms
- Mid-Point Theorem: midpoint segment = ½ of parallel side
