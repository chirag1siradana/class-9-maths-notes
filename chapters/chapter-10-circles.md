# Chapter 10: Circles

## Overview

This chapter covers properties of circles, including chords, arcs, and angles subtended by them.

---

## Key Concepts

### 10.1 Basic Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Circle | Set of all points equidistant from a fixed point (centre) |
| Radius | Distance from centre to any point on circle |
| Diameter | Longest chord; twice the radius (d = 2r) |
| Chord | Line segment joining two points on the circle |
| Arc | Part of the circle between two points |
| Sector | Region between two radii and an arc |
| Segment | Region between a chord and its arc |
| Tangent | Line touching the circle at exactly one point |

### 10.2 Chord Properties
- **Theorem:** Equal chords are equidistant from the centre.
- **Theorem:** Chords equidistant from the centre are equal.
- **Theorem:** The perpendicular from the centre to a chord bisects the chord.
- **Theorem:** The line drawn through the centre to bisect a chord is perpendicular to the chord.

### 10.3 Angles Subtended by a Chord
- **Theorem:** Equal chords subtend equal angles at the centre.
- **Theorem:** Chords that subtend equal angles at the centre are equal.

### 10.4 Angles Subtended by an Arc
- **Theorem:** The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is **twice** the angle it subtends at any point on the remaining part of the circle.
- Angle in a semicircle = **90°**.

### 10.5 Cyclic Quadrilateral
- A quadrilateral whose vertices all lie on a circle.
- **Theorem:** The sum of either pair of opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral is **180°** (they are supplementary).

---

## Important Theorems

1. Equal chords of a circle subtend equal angles at the centre.
2. Perpendicular from centre to a chord bisects it.
3. Angle subtended at centre = 2 × angle subtended at circle.
4. Angles in the same segment are equal.
5. Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°.

---

## Examples

**Example 1:** An arc subtends an angle of 80° at the centre. What angle does it subtend at the circle?
> Angle at circle = 80°/2 = **40°**

**Example 2:** In a cyclic quadrilateral, one angle is 110°. Find the opposite angle.
> 180° − 110° = **70°**

**Example 3:** A chord of a circle is 8 cm. The radius is 5 cm. Find the distance from the centre to the chord.
> Half chord = 4 cm; distance = √(5² − 4²) = √9 = **3 cm**

---

## Practice Problems

1. AB and CD are equal chords of a circle. Prove they are equidistant from the centre.
2. Prove that angles in the same segment are equal.
3. In a circle, if arc PQ subtends ∠POQ = 100° at centre O, find ∠PAQ where A is on the major arc.
4. ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral. If ∠A = 85°, find ∠C.
5. A diameter of a circle subtends an angle at the circumference. What is this angle?

---

## Summary

- Key terms: radius, diameter, chord, arc, sector, segment
- Perpendicular from centre bisects chord
- Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle (same arc)
- Angles in the same segment are equal
- Opposite angles of cyclic quadrilateral = 180°
