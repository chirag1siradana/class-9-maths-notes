# Class 9 Maths – Revision Notes

Quick-revision summaries for all 15 chapters, designed for last-minute review before exams.

---

## Chapter 1 – Number Systems
- ℝ = Rationals (p/q) ∪ Irrationals (non-terminating, non-repeating)
- Rationalise: multiply by conjugate
- Key: (√a + √b)(√a − √b) = a − b
- Exponent laws: aᵐ·aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ; (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ; a⁰ = 1

---

## Chapter 2 – Polynomials
- Types by degree: constant (0), linear (1), quadratic (2), cubic (3)
- Remainder Theorem: p(x) ÷ (x − a) → remainder = p(a)
- Factor Theorem: (x − a) is a factor ⟺ p(a) = 0
- Must-know: (a±b)², (a±b)³, a²−b², a³±b³, (a+b+c)²

---

## Chapter 3 – Coordinate Geometry
- x-axis (horizontal), y-axis (vertical), origin (0,0)
- Quadrants: I(+,+), II(−,+), III(−,−), IV(+,−)
- Points on x-axis: (a, 0); on y-axis: (0, b)

---

## Chapter 4 – Linear Equations in Two Variables
- Form: ax + by + c = 0; infinite solutions
- Each solution = a point on the line
- x = a → vertical line; y = b → horizontal line

---

## Chapter 5 – Euclid's Geometry
- Axioms (general) vs Postulates (geometric)
- 5 Postulates; Postulate 5 = Parallel Postulate
- Theorem = proved using axioms + postulates + definitions

---

## Chapter 6 – Lines and Angles
- Complementary = 90°; Supplementary = 180°; Vertically opposite = equal
- Parallel + transversal: corresponding = equal; alternate = equal; co-interior = 180°
- Angle sum of △ = 180°

---

## Chapter 7 – Triangles
- Congruence: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, RHS
- Isosceles △: equal sides ↔ equal angles
- Triangle inequality: a + b > c for every combination

---

## Chapter 8 – Quadrilaterals
- Sum of angles = 360°
- Parallelogram: opp sides equal, opp angles equal, diagonals bisect each other
- Rectangle: equal diagonals; Rhombus: ⊥ diagonals; Square: both
- Mid-Point Theorem: segment joining midpoints = ½ third side, parallel to it

---

## Chapter 9 – Areas of Parallelograms and Triangles
- Same base + same parallels → equal areas
- ar(triangle) = ½ × ar(parallelogram) — same base and parallels
- Median splits triangle into two equal-area triangles

---

## Chapter 10 – Circles
- Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle (same arc)
- Angles in the same segment are equal
- ⊥ from centre bisects chord
- Cyclic quadrilateral: opposite angles are supplementary

---

## Chapter 11 – Constructions
- Tools: compass + straightedge only
- Perpendicular bisector of a segment
- Angle bisector
- Angles: 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, 120°
- Triangles: SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS

---

## Chapter 12 – Heron's Formula
- s = (a + b + c) / 2
- Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]
- Equilateral: (√3/4)a²

---

## Chapter 13 – Surface Areas and Volumes
| Solid | TSA | Volume |
|-------|-----|--------|
| Cuboid | 2(lb+bh+hl) | lbh |
| Cube | 6a² | a³ |
| Cylinder | 2πr(r+h) | πr²h |
| Cone | πr(r+l) | ⅓πr²h |
| Sphere | 4πr² | (4/3)πr³ |
| Hemisphere | 3πr² | (2/3)πr³ |

Slant height of cone: l = √(r² + h²)

---

## Chapter 14 – Statistics
- Mean = Σx/n
- Median = middle value (arranged in order)
- Mode = most frequent value
- Graphs: bar graph, histogram, frequency polygon

---

## Chapter 15 – Probability
- P(E) = favourable trials / total trials
- 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1
- P(E) + P(not E) = 1
- P = 0: impossible; P = 1: certain
